# frontend-design Skill Overview

This skill guides creation of distinctive, production-grade frontend interfaces that avoid generic aesthetics. The core principle is committing to a **BOLD aesthetic direction** before coding—whether brutally minimal, maximalist, retro-futuristic, or another intentional style.

## Key Design Principles

**Before coding**, understand:
- Purpose and audience
- Tonal direction (extreme aesthetic choices work better than middle ground)
- Technical constraints
- One memorable differentiator

**Implementation must be**:
- Functional and production-ready
- Visually striking with clear aesthetic intent
- Cohesive throughout all details

## Critical Aesthetic Guidance

The skill emphasizes avoiding "generic AI slop" by rejecting:
- "Generic fonts like Arial and Inter" in favor of distinctive, characterful choices
- "Cookie-cutter design that lacks context-specific character"
- "Cliched color schemes (particularly purple gradients on white backgrounds)"
- "Predictable layouts and component patterns"

**Focus areas include**:
- Distinctive typography pairing display and body fonts
- Cohesive color systems with dominant colors and sharp accents
- Strategic animation for high-impact moments
- Unexpected spatial composition (asymmetry, overlap, grid-breaking)
- Atmospheric details matching the overall aesthetic vision

The core message: intentionality matters more than intensity. "Elegance comes from executing the vision well."
